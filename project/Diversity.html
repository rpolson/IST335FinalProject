Diversity

Diversity can be defined as a characteristic of a group if people where differences exist on one or more relevant dimensions. Many programs have been put in place in order to monitor and set guidelines to be followed such as Affirmative Action and Diversity Management in order to help organizations ensure their employee pool is one that is diverse.

Affirmative action is a program that’s purpose is to prevent and/or remedy discrimination of any kind. It assumes individuals will individually assimilate into the organization but focuses more on the recruitment, mobility, and retention aspect of employee-employer relations. The problem with affirmative action is it doesn’t address the cause of problems. Rather, it targets individuals identified as disadvantaged and ensures that they are getting equal opportunities.

Diversity Management aims to create an inclusive work environment where all associates are empowered to perform their best. This assumes that managers and the organizations will change. The focus is to create an environment that allows all associates to reach their full potential. This is targeted at all associate not just those from a minority.
